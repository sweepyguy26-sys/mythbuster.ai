Common Myths & Facts

Common Myths

"Humans only use 10% of their brain capacity."

Modern neuroscience shows we use virtually all parts of our brain.

"Shaving makes hair grow back thicker and darker."

Shaving doesn't change hair thickness or color, it just creates a blunt tip.

"Goldfish have a 3-second memory span."

Goldfish can remember things for months and can be trained.

"You need to wait 24 hours before reporting a missing person."

You should report missing persons immediately to increase chances of finding them.

Verified Facts

"Regular exercise improves brain function and memory."

Studies show exercise increases blood flow to the brain and promotes neurogenesis.

"Drinking 8 glasses of water daily is beneficial for health."

Proper hydration supports kidney function, skin health, and overall bodily processes.

"Vaccines have significantly reduced infectious disease mortality."

Vaccines prevent 2-3 million deaths annually according to WHO data.

"The Great Wall of China is not visible from space with the naked eye."

NASA confirms it's not visible without aid, contrary to popular belief.
